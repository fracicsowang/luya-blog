Avocado toast earned its fame for good reason. It is fast, it is creamy, and it photographs beautifully. But if your toast routine has settled into the same green mash every morning, your taste buds may be quietly begging for a change. The fix is small, fresh, and sitting right on your counter: microgreens.

A handful of these tiny seedlings does more than make a slice of bread look like it belongs in a cafe. They add crunch, a burst of peppery or nutty flavor, and a real nutritional upgrade. Research from the University of Maryland and the USDA found that many microgreens carry far higher concentrations of vitamins than the full-grown versions of the same plant — in some cases dramatically so. So a pinch on your toast is doing quiet, useful work.

Below are five toast combinations that go well past avocado, each paired with the microgreen that flatters it most. Think of this as a starting map, not a rulebook. Once you understand which flavors play together, you will start improvising.

## Why microgreens belong on toast in the first place

Toast is a blank canvas with one weakness: it can feel flat. A creamy or salty topping needs something to cut against it, both in flavor and in texture. Microgreens solve both problems at once.

They bring living crunch that a wilted lettuce leaf never could, and their flavors range from sweet and nutty to sharp and peppery. That range is what makes them so easy to pair — there is a microgreen for almost any topping. If you want a deeper tour of those tastes, our guide to [microgreen flavor profiles](/microgreens-flavor-profiles/) breaks down what to expect from each variety.

There is also the freshness factor. Microgreens are at their best within minutes of being cut, which is exactly why store-bought clamshells so often disappoint — by the time they reach your kitchen, the clock has been running for days. Snipping them yourself, as we cover in [how to eat microgreens](/how-to-eat-microgreens/), keeps the texture and the nutrients intact.

## The classic, upgraded: avocado toast with radish microgreens

We are not abandoning avocado — we are improving it. Mashed avocado is rich and mild, which means it can taste one-note on its own. Radish microgreens are the antidote. They carry a genuine peppery kick, sometimes as sharp as a mature radish, and that heat cuts straight through the creaminess.

Mash half an avocado with a squeeze of lime and a pinch of salt, spread it thick, then pile on a small handful of radish microgreens. A few flakes of chili or a crack of black pepper finishes it. The darker red and purple radish varieties look striking here and tend to carry the most antioxidants, so you eat with your eyes and your health at once. Radish also happens to be one of the [easiest microgreens to grow](/easiest-microgreens-to-grow/), which makes this an easy habit to keep.

## Ricotta toast with pea shoots

Soft, milky ricotta is one of the most underrated toast bases. It is creamy without being heavy, slightly sweet, and it loves a fresh green topping. Pea shoots are the natural match — they taste like the first bite of spring, sweet and faintly grassy, with a satisfying snap.

Spread the ricotta generously, drizzle with good olive oil and a little honey or lemon zest, and finish with a tangle of [pea shoots](/pea-shoots/). The sweetness of the shoots echoes the ricotta rather than fighting it, and the texture keeps each bite interesting. This one works equally well as a quick breakfast or a light afternoon snack.

## Smoked salmon toast with mild, herby greens

Smoked salmon toast is the brunch heavyweight: salty, silky, and a little luxurious. It needs a topping that adds freshness without competing for attention. Skip anything too peppery here and reach for milder, herb-leaning microgreens — cilantro brings a citrusy lift, and parsley adds a clean, fruity note without the fibrous stems of the mature herb.

Layer cream cheese or crème fraîche on the toast, drape over the salmon, add thin red onion if you like, and scatter the microgreens last so they stay perky. A squeeze of lemon ties it together. The microgreens here act the way a chef uses them — as a bright finishing accent rather than a bulk ingredient, a technique we explore in [microgreens garnish like a chef](/microgreens-garnish-like-a-chef/).

## Hummus toast with sunflower microgreens

Hummus on toast is earthy, nutty, and filling — a fantastic savory option that happens to be plant-based. Sunflower microgreens are its perfect companion. They are sweet, distinctly nutty, and have a thick, juicy crunch that stands up beautifully to the dense chickpea spread.

Spread the hummus thick, add a swirl of olive oil and a dusting of paprika or sumac, then heap on the sunflower greens. For extra heft, top with sliced cucumber or roasted red pepper. This combination is genuinely satisfying, and because both elements are protein-friendly, it makes a smart choice for anyone leaning toward a [vegan microgreens diet](/microgreens-vegan-diet/). For more on the most filling varieties, see our notes on [high-protein microgreens](/high-protein-microgreens/).

## Egg toast with broccoli microgreens

A fried or soft-boiled egg on toast is comfort food at its most basic, and a little dull greenery transforms it into something you would happily serve a guest. Broccoli microgreens are the standout choice. They are mild and slightly bitter with a pleasant crunch, and they are the most studied microgreen of all — rich in sulforaphane, the compound behind much of broccoli's reputation for health benefits.

Toast your bread, add the egg, season well, and finish with a handful of broccoli microgreens while the yolk is still warm so they soften just slightly. If you want to understand why this particular green gets so much attention, our piece on [broccoli microgreens](/broccoli-microgreens/) and our explainer on [what is sulforaphane](/what-is-sulforaphane/) go deeper.

## A quick pairing cheat sheet

When you are standing at the counter deciding what to make, this table sums up the logic. The idea is balance: match a rich base with a sharp green, and a salty base with a milder one.

| Toast base | Best microgreen | Flavor of the green | Why it works |
|---|---|---|---|
| Avocado | Radish | Sweet, peppery, sharp | Heat cuts the creamy fat |
| Ricotta | Pea shoots | Sweet, grassy, snappy | Sweetness echoes the cheese |
| Smoked salmon | Cilantro or parsley | Citrusy, mild, fruity | Fresh lift without overpowering |
| Hummus | Sunflower | Sweet, nutty, juicy crunch | Nutty notes mirror the chickpeas |
| Egg | Broccoli | Mild, slightly bitter, crunchy | Gentle bite balances rich yolk |

## What a handful actually adds

Microgreens are small, so it is fair to ask whether a pinch makes any real difference. The answer is that they are concentrated. Many varieties pack several times the nutrient density of their mature forms, which is why a modest topping carries more weight than its size suggests. Here is a rough sense of what different greens bring to the table.

| Microgreen | Notable nutrients | Stands out for |
|---|---|---|
| Radish | Vitamins C, E, K; antioxidants | Color and antioxidant content |
| Broccoli | Sulforaphane; vitamins, minerals | Most-researched health profile |
| Cilantro | Lutein, beta-carotene | Eye-supporting carotenoids |
| Red cabbage | Vitamins C, E, K | Among the richest in vitamins |
| Sunflower | Plant protein, healthy fats | Texture and staying power |

These numbers are why nutrition researchers keep circling back to microgreens. If you want the full picture, our overview of [microgreens nutrition](/microgreens-nutrition/) lays out the science in plain terms.

## The takeaway

Avocado toast was only ever the beginning. Once you start matching microgreens to your favorite toppings — peppery radish on avocado, sweet pea shoots on ricotta, nutty sunflower on hummus — toast stops being a default and becomes something you actually look forward to. The greens add crunch, color, and a real dose of nutrition, all from a handful that takes seconds to add.

Pick one combination tomorrow morning. Then start improvising. Your toast, and your taste buds, have a lot of upgrading left to do.
